Written Objection — Full Text

Submitted Pursuant to M.G.L. Chapter 87, Sections 3 & 4

TO: Leah Grigorov, Tree Warden / Conservation Agent Department of Public Works, 170 Dwight Road, Longmeadow, MA 01106
Longmeadow Select Board: Josh Levine (Chair), Vineeth Hemavathi (Vice Chair), Shelly Maynard-DeWolf (Clerk), Andrew Lam, Dan Zwirko Greenwood Municipal Center, 231 Maple Road, Longmeadow, MA 01106
Lyn Simmons, Town Manager & Longmeadow Tree Committee Greenwood Municipal Center, 231 Maple Road, Longmeadow, MA 01106
RE: Formal Written Objection to the Proposed Cutting or Removal of Public Shade Trees on Williams Street — M.G.L. c. 87, § 4

Dear Tree Warden Grigorov, Members of the Select Board, Town Manager Simmons, and Members of the Tree Committee:

We, the undersigned residents and abutters of Williams Street and the surrounding neighborhood in Longmeadow, Massachusetts, hereby submit this formal written objection to the proposed cutting or removal of public shade trees on Williams Street.

This objection is submitted pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 87, Section 4, which provides:

"Tree wardens shall not cut down or remove or grant a permit for the cutting down or removal of a public shade tree if, at or before a public hearing as provided in the preceding section, objection in writing is made by one or more persons, unless such cutting or removal or permit to cut or remove is approved by the selectmen or by the mayor."

By operation of this statute, the submission of this written objection by one or more of the undersigned triggers a mandatory stay of the proposed cutting or removal. The Tree Warden is thereby prohibited from proceeding with the removal of any affected public shade tree on Williams Street unless and until the Longmeadow Select Board has reviewed the matter and granted its approval at a duly noticed public hearing.

The undersigned object to the proposed tree removal on the following grounds:

  1. The trees in question are mature public shade trees that provide substantial aesthetic, environmental, and economic value to the residents of Williams Street and the broader community.
  2. The removal of these trees will cause irreparable harm to the character of the neighborhood, including the reduction of canopy cover, loss of wildlife habitat, decreased property values, and increased heat and stormwater runoff.
  3. Adequate justification for removal has not been established. The undersigned are not satisfied that the trees pose a documented safety hazard or other condition warranting permanent removal rather than maintenance or remediation.
  4. Residents were not afforded a meaningful opportunity to participate in the decision-making process prior to the proposed removal.
  5. The public interest strongly favors preservation of these trees absent a compelling and well-documented basis for removal.

The undersigned respectfully request that the Longmeadow Select Board:

  1. Issue an immediate halt to any planned cutting or removal of public shade trees on Williams Street, pending Select Board review;
  2. Schedule a public hearing at which affected residents may present testimony and evidence;
  3. Require the Tree Warden to provide a written statement of the specific justification for each proposed removal; and
  4. Fully consider all alternatives to removal, including trimming, treatment, root management, or other tree care remedies.

We further note that M.G.L. Chapter 87, Section 6 provides penalties for violations of Sections 3 and 4, and that any person whose property is injured by an unlawful tree removal may seek damages from the Town under M.G.L. Chapter 79. We raise these provisions not as a threat, but to underscore the seriousness with which the law treats the removal of public shade trees.

We respectfully urge the Town to honor both the letter and spirit of Chapter 87 and to work collaboratively with the Williams Street community toward an outcome that protects these irreplaceable trees.

Respectfully submitted,
