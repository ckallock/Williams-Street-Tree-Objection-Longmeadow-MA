RE: Citizen Petition for Comprehensive Performance Review and Non-Reappointment of Tree Warden Leah Grigorov — Pattern of Procedural Non-Compliance, Lack of Scientific Rigor, and Failure of Public Accountability

We, the undersigned residents and taxpayers of the Town of Longmeadow, formally petition the Town Manager to conduct a comprehensive performance review of Tree Warden Leah Grigorov and to exercise the Town's personnel authority to decline her reappointment for the upcoming term. This petition is grounded in a documented pattern of procedural non-compliance, a lack of scientific rigor in tree assessments, disregard for resident rights to information and due process, and decisions that conflict with the Town's publicly funded canopy preservation goals.

The Williams Street tree removal proposal is the most recent and visible example of these concerns, but this petition addresses the Tree Warden's overall conduct and fitness for reappointment during her tenure. This petition is submitted alongside our concurrent Written Objection under M.G.L. c. 87, § 4, which has already triggered a mandatory stay of the proposed Williams Street removals.

II. Grounds for Review and Non-Reappointment

1. Pattern of Bypassing Required Public Notice Procedures

M.G.L. c. 87, § 3 imposes mandatory notice requirements before any public shade tree may be removed. The undersigned have observed a recurring pattern in which these requirements have not been fully satisfied. The Williams Street proposal is the most recent example — residents were not afforded meaningful advance notice or a genuine opportunity to participate before removal decisions were made.

2. Lack of Scientific Rigor — Overreliance on Subjective Visual Assessment

The Tree Warden has consistently failed to provide written ISA risk assessments or empirical diagnostic documentation to support removal recommendations. The trees proposed on Williams Street appear visibly healthy. Reliance on subjective visual assessment alone — without tools such as Sonic Tomography or Resistograph testing — does not meet the professional standard expected of a qualified arborist recommending permanent removal of mature public trees.

3. Failure to Respond to Resident Complaints and Requests for Documentation

Residents have repeatedly sought access to written assessment reports, risk ratings, and the professional basis for specific removal decisions. These requests have not been adequately addressed. The failure to proactively provide — or to make available upon request — the documentation underlying removal recommendations is a failure of basic public accountability that denies residents their right to meaningful participation in the statutory hearing process.

4. Decisions That Conflict with Town-Funded Canopy Goals

In 2022, Longmeadow received a $20,000 DCR Urban Forestry grant to assess and preserve its 9,000 street trees, valued at over $1.6 million in annual ecosystem services. The Tree Warden's pattern of recommending removal of mature, healthy-appearing trees without documented justification directly contradicts these publicly funded commitments and works against the Town's own environmental goals.

5. Statutory Qualification Requirements — M.G.L. Chapter 41, Section 106

For towns exceeding 10,000 inhabitants, the Tree Warden must be "qualified by training and experience in the field of arboriculture and licensed with the Department of Food and Agriculture." The undersigned request that the Town Manager confirm on the record that Tree Warden Grigorov meets all requirements under M.G.L. c. 41, § 106 and that her assessments use ISA-recognized methodology. In light of the concerns raised here, this confirmation is essential to the Town's due diligence.

III. Actions Requested of the Town Manager

  1. Conduct a comprehensive performance review of Tree Warden Leah Grigorov covering her overall tenure and present findings to the Select Board at a duly noticed public meeting;
  2. Decline to reappoint Tree Warden Grigorov for the upcoming term and initiate a search for a successor holding current ISA or Massachusetts Certified Arborist credentials;
  3. Suspend all non-emergency tree removals on Williams Street pending an independent third-party arborist review using empirical diagnostic methods;
  4. Require written ISA risk assessments for each tree proposed for removal on Williams Street, including defects observed, risk rating, diagnostic methodology, and basis for removal over remediation;
  5. Establish a policy requiring all future removal recommendations to be supported by written, empirically grounded assessments available to residents in advance of any hearing;
  6. Confirm on the record that the Tree Warden meets all M.G.L. c. 41, § 106 qualification requirements; and
  7. Respond to this petition in writing within 14 days and place this matter on the Select Board's next public meeting agenda.
